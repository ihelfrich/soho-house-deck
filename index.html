Soho House.
What the numbers say.

An independent, primary-source investigation into the firm, its money, its owners, and its real estate.
Method & disclaimer

Evidence, tiered. Not allegation.

This is analysis of public records: 303 SEC filings, NYC and UK property registries, the take-private proxy, the corporate-charges registers. It was built with six custom forensic tools under one rule.

Every claim is tiered: T1 verified against a primary source, T2 strong, T3 a lead, T4 interpretation. Nothing here alleges a crime. The findings concern structure and power, not wrongdoing, and the careful framing is deliberate.

The thesis
A loved brand, wrapped around a company structured so that a measurable stream of member money flows, as rent, to its own controlling shareholder. In January 2026 that shareholder took it private, ending the disclosure that showed it.

The take-private

A price fixed early, a vote that was a formality.

  • The $9.00 price was reached August 2024, seventeen months before close, and never moved. T1
  • The Reinvestment Stockholders held 97.8% of the voting power, bound to vote yes. T1
  • Morgan Stanley's own sum-of-the-parts analysis valued the shares at $5.77 to $7.85, below the deal price. T1
The UK layer

The debt history, read off the public register.

The UK Companies House charges register records every mortgage over the British operating entities: Bank of Scotland in the Caring years, then Wells Fargo, then Glas Trust.

The take-private financing is visible: a cluster of new charges registered across the UK entities on 29 January 2026, the exact closing date. T1

The Abu Dhabi dimension

A director of Soho House now runs its competitor.

In April 2026, Abu Dhabi's International Holding Company, through its hospitality arm DIAFA, bought a majority of Richard Caring's separate empire, the Ivy Collection and the Birley Clubs including Annabel's, for about £1.4bn. T1

Soho House was not part of that deal. But Caring sits on Soho House's board and now chairs the Abu Dhabi vehicle that owns Soho House's storied London competitors. A conflict of position, not shared ownership.

The network

A concentrated structure, not a web.

Competitors are structural islands. The single bridge is one person: Richard Caring. T2

The brand

The demand is real. The value proposition is slipping.

Demand is not in question: the waitlist roughly doubled past 100,000; membership reached about 270,000. T2

But members are paying more to get in and spending less once inside. The brand still pulls; the deal inside it is thinning.

Intellectual honesty

What this will not overclaim.

Nothing here is evidence of a crime. The related-party leases, the Adjusted EBITDA, the take-private: all disclosed, because the SEC required it. The finding is about structure and power.

Red flags are catalogued as questions, never asserted as wrongdoing. One propagated error was caught and corrected on the record. That correction is part of the method.
